    Introduction

    The housing price in 2021 has a dramatic increase compared with the previous year. The market still shows no signs of slowing down. There are mixed factors that contribute to the price increase over the past year. The factors include the imbalance between supply and demand, a historically low mortgage rate, a high inflation rate, the volatile stock market, and so on.

    My goal for this project is to investigate what factors have a greater impact on home appreciation. In other words, which types of homes are more popular and make the buyers willing to pay more? Is it because it's in a highly desirable area, or it has a big lot size? This information may be useful to first-time homebuyers.

    Because sales volume has a strong seasonal pattern, using the data of a single month may not reflect the actual trends. I used Redfin data from 2020Q4 to 2021Q4. The regions include counties in the Bay Area except for the counties in the North Bay: Alameda, Santa Clara, Contra Costa, San Mateo, and San Francisco. Single-family homes, townhouses, and condos will be discussed separately.

    Things to be explored:

    • Discover the distribution of median sold price, unit sold price per sqft, YoY change of median sold price, and average sale-to-list ratio by zip code.
    • Identify if lot size or living space has an impact on the sold price of single families.
    • Identify if year built or monthly HOA has an impact on the sold price of townhouses.

    Conclusions

    This visualization work helps the target audience to have a better understanding of the housing market in the bay area. One drawback with the analysis of influencing factors is that by focusing on the selected variables, we are neglecting other factors, such as the condition of the house, the community environment, the school ratings, etc.
