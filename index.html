    Introduction

    The housing price in 2021 has a dramatic increase compared with the previous year. The market still shows no signs of slowing down. There are mixed factors that contribute to the price increase over the past year. The factors include the imbalance between supply and demand, a historically low mortgage rate, a high inflation rate, the volatile stock market, and so on.

    My goal for this project is to investigate what factors have a greater impact on home appreciation. In other words, which types of homes are more popular and make the buyers willing to pay more? Is it because it's in a highly desirable area, or it has a big lot size? This information may be useful to first-time homebuyers.

    Because sales volume has a strong seasonal pattern, using the data of a single month may not reflect the actual trends. I used Redfin data from 2020Q4 to 2021Q4. The regions include counties in the Bay Area except for the counties in the North Bay: Alameda, Santa Clara, Contra Costa, San Mateo, and San Francisco. Single-family homes, townhouses, and condos will be discussed separately.

    Things to be explored:

    • Discover the distribution of median sale price, unit sold price per sqft, YoY change of median sold price, and average sale-to-list ratio by zip code.
    • Identify if lot size or living space has an impact on the sold price of single families.
    • Identify if year built or monthly HOA has an impact on the sold price of townhouses.

If you are a buyer who prefers single families built after 2000, there are not many listings in the market. However, if you consider purchasing townhouses or condos, many of them are built after 2000.

    Linear Regression

    R-squared is the coefficient of determination. It has a value between 0 to 1 and measures the total variance explained by the model. The closer R-squared is to 1, the more it indicates that the x-axis variable can explain the y-axis variable.

    From the linear regressions, we can tell that R-squared is too low for lot size. It means that lot size has little explanation over the variance of the sold price. Living space seems to be a better independent variable(R-squared is between 0.5 and 0.8 for most cities).

    Does monthly HOA or year-built have a significant impact on the sold price of townhouses?

    Analyze the median sold price increase of each city and check if the increase has any correlation with:

  1. year-built(older townhouses are less favorable)
  2. monthly HOA (higher HOA makes the homes less desired)

    3. Note: You can drag and select a sliding window on any of the axes shown above. You can remove the window by clicking anywhere else on the selected axis. You can also draw multiple sliding windows(one for each axis) for crossfilter.

    Findings: The interwoven paths between hoa and price indicate that there is no correlation between monthly HOA and townhouse sold price. There are more parallel paths between price and yearBuilt, which means newer townhouses are usually sold at higher prices. Similarly, paths are parallel between yearBuilt and sqft, indicating that newer townhouses are often built with a larger living space.

    For this reason, I decided to give up my previous assumption that HOA may have an impact on townhouse sold price. I only focused on year built and used a slop graph to see if newer homes have a bigger price increase from 2020Q4 to 2021Q4.

    Findings: Except for Campbell, all the other cities share the same trends:

    1. Newer homes have a higher median sold price both in 2020Q4 and 2021Q4.
    2. Newer homes have a bigger slope compared with homes that are built before 2000, which means newer homes have a higher increase in median sold price.

    Note: You can switch to other cities from the drop-down menu above.

    Since the median price does not reflect all the information, I added another chart to show q1 and q3 (25% quartile and 75% quartile of all data points) of the sold price and compared the change from 2020Q4 to 2021Q4. Findings:

    1. The distance between q1 and q3 is large for most cities and year groups, which means that the middle 50% of sold prices have a wide range.
    2. There is an obvious trend of upward shifting within each year group. If you choose San Jose, for townhouses built between 2010 and 2021, even the 25% quartile sold price in 2021Q4 is higher than the 75% quartile sold price in 2020Q4.

    Conclusions

    This visualization work helps the target audience to have a better understanding of the housing market in the bay area. One drawback with the analysis of influencing factors is that by focusing on the selected variables, we are neglecting other factors, such as the condition of the house, the community environment, the school ratings, etc.
